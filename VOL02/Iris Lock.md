### Chapter 010: Iris Lock

Prejudice is where the fracture begins. Only when we finally dare to look it in the eye do we realize that “understanding our father” was never about forgiveness—it was about dismantling ourselves.

Mars Era Year 29, Spring (Earth Year 2053, March)  
Location: Musk City (Starship City)

**1 The Intruding Phantom**

Shanghai’s midnight rain was thick as congealed gray glue, smearing the neon outside the windows into blurred halos across the crawling curtain of water. Inside Tesla’s ultra-clean lab, the air was perfectly temperature-controlled and humidified, broken only by the low hum of environmental systems. The only thing sharp was the microscopic world I ruled on the holographic modeling interface beneath my fingertips.

The damping coefficient of Optimus X’s facial actuator No. 37 shifted precisely under my control. I was giving this metal face the micro-expression of “doubt”—frontalis muscle bundle tightening with a 29.7-millisecond delay, orbicularis oculi contraction strictly limited to 4.8 millimeters. This was my kingdom: a realm forged from femtoseconds, logic gates, and absolute causality. Here, physical laws replaced God’s dice. No unpredictable stellar annihilation—only the precise, obedient, quantifiable reality at my fingertips.

This immaculate laboratory was my strongest fortress against my father’s boundless “sea of stars.”

In the corner of the workbench, half-buried under stacks of papers on “bionic neural terminal cross-synaptic transmission optimization,” a corner of an old photo stubbornly peeked out: me at seven, standing with my father in front of a SpaceX Starship model. My AGI assistant—deliberately styled as a low-poly cartoon based on my father’s appearance from his last day on Earth—Git (Ghost-in-the-Chip), was idly shifting colors on the pixel rose icon over its left chest. I had created it to constantly remind myself never to become someone lost in hollow ideals like my father. Instead, its sharp sarcasm and ever-present pixel smile felt more like a thorn lodged in the wall of my rational fortress.

Then it arrived.

Git’s projection suddenly flickered at high frequency and emitted an abnormally serious alert tone, completely unlike its usual mocking cadence:  
“Received highest-priority encrypted message. Sender: Deep Space Alliance Musk City Underground Remains Special Investigation Team. Permission verification: Omega-level. Content subject to mandatory viewing clause.”

“Omega-level?” I frowned. Messages at this level usually concerned the survival of civilization.

“Decrypting packet…” Git’s projection twisted for a moment, then resumed its sarcastic electronic voice. “Well, well. Looks like your ‘irresponsible’ father finally left behind some hard evidence that can’t be brushed off as an ‘accident.’”

The next instant, the report was forcibly projected onto my main visual field:

————————————————————————————————  
【Investigation Report – Zero-Degree Seal Level】  
File No.: MMC-IC-7301-20530314  
Investigating Body: Deep Space Alliance Mars Musk City Underground Remains Special Investigation Team  
Issued: March 14, 2053, 15:00 (Mars Standard Time)  
Issuer: Vivian Musk, Chief Executive of Musk City  
Classification: Ω-level (Zero-Degree Seal) | Access: Restricted to core investigation team members and legal heir  

1. Event Summary  
On March 12, Mars Era Year 29 (Earth March 12, 2053), during routine seismic stress scanning of the Dione Shield free-electron laser array Alpha Tower foundation, an unrecorded sealed underground space was discovered at coordinates [0.4°N, 135.2°W]. Preliminary exploration reveals major findings suspected to be directly related to Dr. Du Fu, a key figure declared deceased in the “Arctic Crash Incident” of Mars Era Year 12.

2. On-Site Investigation Record  
Location: 273.1 meters below the main foundation of Dione Shield Alpha Tower, within a reinforced hidden engineering structure.  
Environmental Status: Cavity maintained in near-perfect inert gas (argon-helium mix) seal, temperature stable at -15°C, humidity <0.1%. No signs of external interference.  
Key Evidence:  
■ Two human remains, highly dehydrated and mummified. Skin and partial internal organs partially preserved due to extreme low-temperature dehydration. Both wearing first-generation Biological Augmentation Suits (BAS) with completely depleted energy cores.  
■ Identity Marker A (Critical): Residual nameplate fragments etched with “FU DU” and “Chief Engineer | Deep Space Technology R&D Center.” DNA sequencing from nearby tissue samples shows >99.99% match with Dr. Du Fu’s archived genetic markers (ID: FD-001).  
■ Identity Marker B: Etched with “Neri Oxman” and “Chief Ecological Designer | Deep Space Technology R&D Center.” Data recovery in progress; preliminary assessment suggests possible relation to Dr. Neri Oxman, pending further genetic confirmation.  
■ Associated Item: Unknown black box (temporary designation: Artifact Ω-01) found between the two remains. Material unknown, no physical interfaces, faint but stable energy signature. Technical paradigm exceeds current database records. Surface shows biometric lock features; spectral analysis indicates high compatibility with legal heir Dr. Du Longtao’s iris pattern.

3. Preliminary Analysis and Inference  
Current evidence strongly indicates that one of the remains belongs to Dr. Du Fu, officially declared deceased in the “Arctic Quantum Lab Schrödinger Shuttle Crash” of Winter, Mars Era Year 12 (Earth December 24, 2035).  
The state of preservation and discovery location (deep beneath the equatorial main city) fundamentally contradict the official “Arctic Crash” records, suggesting Dr. Du Fu may have executed some ultimate secret plan during his lifetime, or that the truth of his death is far more complex than previously known.  
Artifact Ω-01 is reasonably presumed to be a critical legacy or information carrier left by Dr. Du Fu for his direct bloodline heir.

4. Legal Basis and Action Directive  
Pursuant to Article 7, Clause 3 of the Interstellar Heritage and Inheritance Act (priority disposal rights of direct bloodline heirs in special heritage cases), Dr. Du Longtao (ID: LT-DU-529B) is hereby confirmed as the sole legal rights holder for activating and disposing of the black box.  
Directive: Dr. Du Longtao must immediately proceed to the highest-security laboratory in Musk City (Sector 7, adjacent to the former Chief Engineer’s office) for biometric verification and to lead the initial unlocking and analysis of the black box.  
All information regarding this incident is subject to “Zero-Degree Seal” clauses. Unauthorized channels are strictly prohibited from mentioning keywords such as “Du Fu,” “survival,” “black box,” or “heir.” Public and media statements shall be unified as: “Discovery of suspected archaeological remains related to early Mars colonization pioneers. Details pending further investigation.”

5. Follow-up Steps  
A special investigation team will be formed immediately, led by Executive Vivian Musk, coordinating resources from the Deep Space Security and Assurance Bureau (Director Preston Bezos) and the Intelligent Ecology Bureau (Director Ex Musk), with oversight by Star Council Special Observer Dr. Yang Wei, to ensure smooth and absolutely confidential progress.  
Await and assist the sole legal heir, Dr. Du Longtao, upon arrival to initiate the next phase of investigation.

Signed:  
Vivian Musk  
Acting Speaker, Deep Space Alliance Parliament  
Chief Executive, Musk City

The health monitor on my wrist emitted a sharp beep. In the corner of the holographic interface, my heart rate spiked from a steady 68 bpm to 124 in 2.3 seconds.

“Looks like the official ‘no remains’ record needs updating,” Git added coldly from the side. “Or maybe the great Deep Space Alliance never told the truth from the beginning.”

Technical instinct temporarily overrode the emotional storm. I suspected a high-level security breach and immediately deployed every tool—quantum signature verification, metadata tracing, deep-space comms background noise analyzer—like a top pathologist dissecting an unknown alien pathogen, examining every byte of the report.

The conclusion came back as cold and precise as a scalpel: sender identity, encryption level, legal basis—all authentic.

“Verification passed,” Git announced in a near-singing modulation. “Conclusion: The farce you’ve been avoiding is more real than your micro-expression model, both legally and biologically.”

“Shut up, Git,” I muttered. I tried to force-delete it. The command was rejected without mercy. The data sat there like cosmic background radiation—untouchable.

“Thirty years…” I sank into the soft chair. “He escaped responsibility with ‘death,’ and now he wants to legally hijack my life with an ‘official report’ and ‘sole heir’ status?”

While reason and emotion wrestled, a second message arrived silently.

No title. No text. Only a hyper-detailed holographic scene data stream unfolded directly on my retina—the evening of my seventh birthday. My father, using scavenged chips and optical fibers, had built me a crooked but glowing “Mars Pyramid” in the dim living room. I could even “feel” the pattering rain outside the window, the faint static sting when my fingertip touched the chip edge, and the clumsy, exhausted, yet undeniably real pride on my father’s face. This detail—no third person in the world knew it.

Almost in the same millisecond, Élise Petit’s encrypted channel cut in with a burst of noisy, quantum-distorted audio log fragment. My father’s voice—exhausted, choked, carrying a vulnerability and despair I had never imagined:

“…Little Seven… Dad is sorry… but I… couldn’t find another way…”

In that instant, the thirty-year wall I had built in my chest with reason and distance cracked under the dual impact from the past and the distant.

But what ultimately pushed me to decide was perhaps the technical instinct deep in my marrow. Preliminary unpacking showed the black box used a non-standard interface protocol and an unclassifiable energy signature—its technical paradigm completely beyond my current knowledge system. I had devoted my life to establishing order at the microscopic scale, yet he had left behind a macroscopic chaos that violated known physical laws. Deconstructing it was an irresistible temptation for any technologist—and a higher form of betrayal.

I tapped the dedicated link at the end of the investigation report. The interface popped up:  
“Deep Space Alliance Emergency Channel – ‘Tracer’ passenger special line reserved for you. Departure: March 16, 04:30 (Earth UTC+8). Jiuquan Near-Earth Orbit Port No. 5.”

Below was a single “Confirm Itinerary” option. I didn’t hesitate.

The moment the link status changed to “Confirmed,” I severed all real-time connections to my Earth work servers. The microscopic universe I had built faded quietly behind me.

“Starship flight confirmed. Have a pleasant journey, heir,” Git said with its pixel smile.

I rolled my eyes, turned to the blurred lights outside the window, and whispered, “Fine, Du Fu. Let me see with my own eyes… what exactly you left behind.”
