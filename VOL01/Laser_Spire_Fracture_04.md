### 4.Rules of the Ice Wall

Élise’s technical logic had temporarily repelled Earth’s naked political accusations, but the air in the conference room did not lighten because of it. The gloomy look in Dr. Yang Wei’s eyes as his hologram faded signaled that Earth’s intervention would not end here—it would merely shift to a more “compliant” method. The battle over technological sovereignty paused for the moment, but another war—more covert, governed by colder rules—had already quietly begun.

Preston Bezos’s gaze swept like a probe across every participant at the round table, finally settling on the conspicuously empty seat representing Blueport City. His knuckles tapped the virtual tabletop twice—not heavily, but with a dull thud that drew all attention back.

“Earth’s accusations are on hold for now; diplomatic channels will handle them.” His voice returned to that emotionless, systematized calm. “But right now we face a more urgent internal issue that needs clarification. Alfred, pull all public market transaction records of Gold Financial Investment Group and its affiliated funds from the moment of the incident until now. Focus on screening financial products with direct or indirect ties to Helios Energy, Gaia Mind, and other core assets—including but not limited to Dione Shield–related patent packages and helium-3 futures contracts.”

“Command confirmed. Data screening in progress…” Alfred responded without delay. Vast financial data streams converged and visualized at the center of the round table. Complex K-line charts, capital flow diagrams, and shareholding change graphs grew and intertwined like living vines.

Vivian Musk frowned. “Preston, is now really the time to chase market fluctuations? The priority is stabilization—”

“Executive,” Preston interrupted, eyes still locked on the rapidly scrolling data, “on Mars, capital flows are themselves strategic intelligence. Especially at a moment like this, abnormal movements of large sums often reveal more than missile trajectories.”

Before he finished, Alfred had already highlighted the anomalies. Several glaring red curves stood out like blades slicing through the otherwise relatively stable chart background.

“Anomalous transaction records detected. Account entity: ‘Polaris Opportunity Fund,’ registered in the Blueport City Free Trade Zone. Ultimate beneficial ownership chain penetrates to entities effectively controlled by Mrs. Charlotte Gold and her son Mizar Gold.”

“The fund initiated large-scale, programmatic divestment of bonds from Helios Energy, Gaia Mind, and others in the seventh minute after the incident (Mars Standard Time)—when public information had not yet spread and the market was in a brief information vacuum. Divestment totaled 33.7% of its holdings, with a transaction value of approximately 4.5 billion MUSD (Mars digital currency pegged 1:1 to USD). The entire operation completed in three minutes, precisely skirting the automatic large-trade alert thresholds.”

“Further analysis shows that during the same window, the fund synchronously sold substantial credit default swap (CDS) products tied to Dione Shield operational stability via affiliated dark-pool platforms. The trading pattern… highly precise, as if it anticipated the risk event.”

The charts clearly showed that this “precise” divestment occurred right before the relevant companies’ stock prices began plunging due to Alcor’s statements and Earth’s accusations—like someone quietly removing a critical keystone moments before the dam burst.

Dead silence filled the room. The air seemed to freeze into ice.

Vivian Musk stared at the data in disbelief, lips trembling slightly. “Charlotte… how could she… this is insider trading! This is betrayal!”

Preston’s face showed no surprise—only the cold certainty of “as expected.” “Not betrayal, Executive. Arbitrage. Capital’s instinct to seek certain returns in uncertainty. The only issue is that the timing of this ‘instinct’ is so precise it invites unavoidable associations.” He turned to me. “Ex, what’s your view? Blueport City’s financial tentacles seem to have sensed the tower’s collapse earlier than our sensors.”

A chill ran through me. Charlotte Gold—Alcor’s stepmother, the other pole in the Gold family’s open and covert struggle. Was this operation mere financial profiteering, or—like Alcor—did she have foreknowledge of the incident, or even… participation? Had the Gold family’s infighting reached the point of sacrificing Mars’ stability?

“Director Bezos,” I said gravely, “we need an explanation from Blueport City.”

“Of course.” Preston immediately opened an encrypted line to Blueport City’s Interstellar Digital Finance Center.

The connection was not to Charlotte Gold herself, but to her chief legal counsel—an elderly man with a rigid expression and impeccable attire. His holographic background was a bookshelf crammed with legal volumes.

“Director Bezos, Executive, Doctor.” The old man inclined his head slightly, tone courteous yet distant. “Regarding your inquiry into the ‘Polaris Opportunity Fund’ transactions, we have been informed. On behalf of Mrs. Gold, I state that all activities of the fund strictly comply with the Mars Financial Markets Regulatory Ordinance and Blueport City Free Trade Zone regulations. All operations are compliant investments based on publicly available information and quantitative models. There is no so-called ‘insider trading’ or improper gain.”

He adjusted his glasses and continued in a flat, unwavering tone: “Pursuant to the Mars Information Freedom Act and client privacy protection clauses, unless you present a formal investigation order issued by the Star Council or the Interstellar Legislative and Research Institute, we are neither authorized nor obligated to disclose details of the fund’s specific investment strategies or decision-making processes. If you have no further requests that comply with legal procedure, this call may conclude.”

A wall built of legal provisions—smooth, gleaming, and ice-cold—rose instantly before us. Their stance was clear: everything compliant, nothing to disclose. Want to dig? Follow the rules. And obtaining an investigation order of that level, in the current political climate, was nearly impossible and would take an extremely long time.

Preston’s mouth curved into a cold smile, but he did not explode. He simply severed the connection calmly. “See? That’s the game under rules. They shield themselves with rules, and we must use rules to pry open the cracks.”

The instant the Blueport legal counsel’s image vanished, Preston’s private communicator flashed. He glanced at it—an ultra-encrypted anonymous message, source untraceable. The content was extremely brief: one timestamp and one line.

【Timestamp: 4 hours before the incident. Location: Blueport City, “Stargazer Tower” private club. Attendees: C.G. and an unidentified technical advisor. Topic: Feasibility of “extreme stress testing” for FLUX architecture.】

C.G.—Charlotte Gold’s initials.

This anonymous tip was like a key suddenly placed in our hands. It offered no hard proof, but it pointed unmistakably in one direction: Charlotte had secretly met someone linked to FLUX architecture hours before the accident!

Preston’s fingertips tapped lightly three times on his coffee cup—his unconscious habit when sinking into deep thought. Then he looked up, eyes sharp as he addressed Vivian and me: “Executive, Dr. Ex, based on this lead, my Security Bureau will conduct ‘safety compliance inspections’ on all Blueport City R&D institutions and consulting firms related to FLUX architecture and helium-3 nuclear fusion constraint-field technology—under the pretext of ‘investigating potential technical risks.’ This falls within my statutory authority. Blueport City cannot refuse on grounds of commercial secrecy.”

Vivian instantly understood his intent—a feint in the open while striking from the shadows. The public justification was impeccable safety checks; the real goal was to approach Charlotte Gold’s inner circle and hunt for clues to that “extreme stress testing.”

“I approve,” Vivian stated immediately. “We must get to the bottom of this.”

“Doctor, your technical team will provide necessary professional support,” Preston said to me, tone flat as if reading from a technical report.

I nodded. Cracks had appeared in capital’s ice wall. And the focus of the investigation had swung back once more—to technology itself, to the unfathomable secrets of the FLUX architecture. The Gold family’s infighting resembled a gorgeous yet brutal ballet performed in a dark chamber, and we were trying to pry open a sliver of the door to glimpse whether, beneath those spinning steps, lay explosives waiting to detonate everything.

Preston did not hesitate. This time the signal bypassed all intermediaries. The moment the line connected, a figure slowly coalesced at the far end of the round table.

Charlotte Gold appeared.

She was not in some austere office, but in a tranquil space filled with natural light. Behind her stretched enormous floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking Blueport City’s orderly skyline. She wore an impeccably tailored charcoal-gray suit, posture composed, holding a steaming cup of tea as if she had just finished morning reading.

“Director Bezos, Executive Musk, Dr. Ex.” Her greeting was calm as still water. Her gaze swept over us, finally settling on me with measured scrutiny. “I believe my legal counsel has already addressed your questions regarding compliance.”

Preston skipped pleasantries and cut straight to the core: “Mrs. Gold, we are concerned with why the ‘Polaris Opportunity Fund’ divested so precisely in the assets before incident information spread.”

Charlotte leaned forward slightly, examined the shared data carefully, then offered a faintly regretful, professional smile.

“Director, I understand your concern. But allow me to clarify two points. First, Alcor Gold has not participated in core group decision-making for many years; his personal investment actions do not represent the position of Gold Investment Group. Second, as Executive Director of the Exchange, my primary duty is to maintain absolute market stability and transparency. An accident that cripples an energy tower is harmful to any rational market participant with no benefit whatsoever. The positions we held suffered massive losses in this event—we are victims, not beneficiaries.”

Her explanation was commercially watertight, instantly undermining any assumption of coordinated wrongdoing.

Then she leaned back, hands elegantly crossed on her lap, tone shifting to a subtly meaningful calm.

“As for Alcor… that boy has harbored deep… resentment toward the family—especially toward Mizar and me—ever since his father made his final decision. He longs to prove himself and sometimes takes rather… aggressive personal actions.” She sighed softly, voice carrying just the right weight of sorrow. “As a mother, I find this very regrettable.”

Half-truth, half-fiction—extremely difficult to disprove. She openly implied Alcor had motive: resentment over lost inheritance, jealousy of his half-brother. By expressing “regret” as a mother, she deftly reframed a possible commercial crime into the emotional, irresponsible revenge of a “problematic stepson.”

Finally her gaze sharpened, tone still even but filled with undeniable pressure.

“On the contrary, Director Bezos, before the cause of the accident is clear, you are rushing to steer the investigation toward finance. Does this mean the Alliance Security Department is attempting to find an external scapegoat to cover up certain… fatal flaws inherent in the system itself?” She paused, letting the weight settle. “I must remind you that the stability of Blueport City Digital Finance Center concerns the asset security of millions of Solar System residents. Any accusation not based on evidence could trigger catastrophic market panic. Are you—and the Deep Space Alliance—prepared to bear that responsibility?”

This was a naked warning—and the highest form of counterattack. She had keenly seized the logical vulnerability in the investigation, questioned its motive as scapegoating for technical failure, and wielded “market panic” as the ultimate weapon.

The channel fell into absolute silence. Charlotte Gold’s hologram inclined her head slightly, then vanished, leaving behind a room full of icy air. Her performance was over—calm, precise, methodical. Thunder in perfect silence.

Preston’s face was dark enough to wring water from. Vivian Musk pressed her lips tightly, clearly struck by the “market panic” warning.

And I felt a chill spread from my spine. Alcor’s arrogance was open flame; Charlotte… she was the undercurrent, the abyss beneath the ice. She not only cleared herself, but successfully threw out a more persuasive, more humanized, and harder-to-prove “family infighting” script.
